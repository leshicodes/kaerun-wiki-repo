# MALANUR BLACKSTAR
### Designation: Living Primary Source (LPS-VII)
### Classification: Nobility (Disinherited), Underdark Exile, Bandit Lord (Former)
### Status: Active Companion. Under Observation.

---

## ENTRY I: INITIAL CONTACT
**Date:** 14th of the Ember Moon, 1247 (Lentrian Calendar)
**Location:** The Gilded Sparrow Tavern, Ventrian Border Province

He sat in a peasant's tavern wearing the posture of a throne room.

I noticed him before I noticed Leena had stopped singing. The Half-Elf bard had drawn the crowd's tears with some historically questionable ballad, but my attention snagged on the figure beside her  a dark elf nursing a drink he hadn't touched, watching the room with the patient assessment of a man counting exits.

He wore a black cloak, heavy and travel-stained, pulled close around his shoulders despite the warmth of the room. The fabric pooled around him like spilled ink. Beneath it, when he shifted, I caught the glint of something silver. Something he did not want seen.

His clothing, what little the cloak revealed, bore the cut of a tailor who once charged by the stitch. The seams spoke of wealth. The patches spoke of its absence. A man dressed for a court that no longer wanted him, wrapped in darkness to hide whatever remained of his former glory.

He introduced himself as "Mal." A truncation. A deliberate shedding of syllables, as if the full name weighed too much to carry in exile.

**Physical Description:**
The subject is a Dark Elf of the Underdark lineages, though his coloration marks him as something apart from his kin: snow-white hair, alabaster skin, irises of deep crimson that catch torchlight like embers drowning in milk. He is gaunt to the point of architecture. His cheekbones could cast shadows at noon. The moniker "The Thin Pale Duke" requires no poetic license. It is simply accurate.

Beneath the cloak  and he keeps the cloak on whenever possible, adjusting it with the unconscious frequency of a man checking a bandage  he wears scaled silver armor trimmed with gold. The armor fits him like a second skin. He cannot remove it. He has tried. (See: ENTRY IV: THE ARMOR AND THE DEMON WITHIN).

*Note on the cloak: He claims it is for warmth. The Underdark breeds creatures poorly suited to surface climates, and this explanation is plausible. It is also incomplete. He hides the armor because the armor draws questions. Questions lead to stories. Stories, for a man with a bounty and a curse, are dangerous currency.*

**Assessment:**
A primary source of immense value. His knowledge of Underdark noble houses  specifically the fall of House Blackstar and the political architecture of Castle Ormen  exists nowhere else. When Velara purged the loyalists, she purged the witnesses. He is the last page of a book his sister tried to burn.

He is also, demonstrably, a man who speaks around wounds rather than through them. The Codex has noted seven instances of deflection when questioned about his disinheritance. The quill has yet to twitch. He is omitting, not falsifying. This is a different sin, and one I am more willing to tolerate.

For now.

*Addendum: He has begun volunteering information  particularly details that cast him in a favorable light. His tactical brilliance during the bandit years. His mercy toward surrendering enemies. The loyalty he inspired in his followers. I have noted this shift. Vanity is a useful lever, and I am not above applying pressure to it when the Archive requires.*

## ENTRY II: THE FALL OF HOUSE BLACKSTAR
**Source:** Subject's own testimony, corroborated against trade records from Kydorian merchant guilds.

Castle Ormen sits somewhere in the Underdark's tangled geography  a fortress of significant historical weight that the subject claims as his birthright. The claim is legally void. The grief behind it is not.

**The Disinheritance:**
Malanur was the elder twin by seven minutes. In most noble traditions, this would have settled the matter. In House Blackstar, it settled nothing.

Their father passed the inheritance to Velara, the younger sister. The subject has described the precipitating incident only as "an unforgivable embarrassment." When I pressed for details, his jaw tightened and his hand drifted toward his sword  not in threat, but in the unconscious gesture of a man reaching for something that is no longer there.

I did not press further. The Codex remains open on this page. Patience has served me better than interrogation.

**The Purge:**
Upon assuming power, Velara moved with commendable efficiency. Every loyalist within Castle Ormen was executed. Servants. Guards. A tutor who had taught both twins their letters. The sister did not merely claim the throne; she salted the earth around it.

Malanur escaped with a small retinue and what he calls "a few family valuables."

*Note: This phrase is doing significant labor. Based on his subsequent activities, this haul funded mercenary inquiries across three kingdoms. It was insufficient, however, to fund a mercenary contract. The distinction between information and action is measured in coin, and he ran out before the killing could begin.*

**Corroboration:**
Kydorian trade ledgers confirm a cessation of exports from Ormen-controlled routes approximately eighteen months before the subject's arrival on the surface. The records do not name Velara, but they note a new seal on the shipping manifests. The old seal bore a comet streaking across darkness. The new seal bears a spider.

The symbolism requires no interpretation.

## ENTRY III: THE BANDIT YEARS
**Source:** Subject's testimony. Partially corroborated by wanted posters in three jurisdictions.

A throne requires an army. An army requires gold. Gold, for a disinherited exile, requires creativity.

The subject approached a bandit clan operating in the mountains west of Kydor. His proposal was simple: his tactical expertise in exchange for a share of their profits and, eventually, their loyalty.

The bandits, displaying the predictable cunning of their profession, seized him instead. A pale elf claiming noble blood? His sister would pay handsomely for his return. Dead or alive was, presumably, negotiable.

**The Coup:**
What the bandits failed to account for was the retinue.

The subject's remaining Underdark companions infiltrated the camp by night and cut him free. What followed  by his own account, delivered with the flat affect of a man describing weather  was calculated brutality. He executed the bandit chieftain personally, in front of the assembled clan. A blade across the throat. No speech. No ceremony. Just the wet sound of authority changing hands.

Then he sheathed his sword and offered the survivors a choice.

They chose to live.

The subject spent several years expanding this clan into something approaching a private army. "The Thin Pale Duke" became a figure whispered about in merchant caravans and cursed in tax collector's offices. He was building something  coin by stolen coin, recruit by desperate recruit. Every theft was a brick in the foundation of a war he could not yet afford to fight.

**Assessment:**
I do not approve. The ledger of his crimes would fill a chapter of its own, and the families he robbed did not care about the nobility of his long-term intentions.

But I understand. Desperation has its own arithmetic, and his numbers were always against him.

## ENTRY IV: THE ARMOR AND THE DEMON WITHIN
**Source:** Subject's testimony, supplemented by observations and Leena's account. Partially verified through divine inquiry.

The scaled silver armor  the thing he hides beneath the black cloak, the thing he adjusts and conceals and never speaks of unless asked  is the axis around which Malanur Blackstar's life now turns.

He cannot remove it. He has tried. The cloak is a bandage over a wound that will not close.

**Acquisition:**
The story begins, as these stories often do, with a man willing to do terrible things for power.

The subject intercepted a carriage carrying priests of an unidentified order. Under duress  and I will not dwell on what form that duress took  they revealed the location of an artifact. A suit of armor, they said, that would grant him the strength to defeat his enemies. To reclaim what was stolen. To bring his greatest enemy to heel.

He believed them. This was his first mistake.

The journey to the artifact's resting place cost him most of his remaining followers. By the time he reached the shrine  if shrine is even the correct word  only two companions remained: a loyalist from Ormen, and one of the captured priests.

The armor waited for him in the dark. Silver scales. Gold trim. And a voice.

**The Voice:**
The subject has described it as ancient. Patient. Amused.

He calls it a demon. He calls it worse than that  I have recorded seventeen distinct epithets, ranging from the profane to the creatively anatomical. The Voice, for its part, does not seem to mind. Or perhaps it enjoys the attention.

It promised him exactly what the priests had promised: that if he donned the armor, his greatest enemy would be brought to heel.

He put it on.

*Personal Addendum: When he reached this point in the narrative, I set down my quill. The Codex hovered, waiting. The silence stretched long enough to become uncomfortable.*

*"I know," he said, before I could speak. "I know."*

**The Binding:**
The armor fused with him on a level that resists physical analysis. I have observed him sleep in it, fight in it, sit in the rain while it beaded on the scales like tears on a statue's face. It has become part of his silhouette, inseparable from the man within  which is precisely why the cloak never leaves his shoulders. He wraps himself in black cloth the way a burn victim wraps themselves in bandages: to hide what lies beneath, to deflect the questions, to pretend for a few hours that he is simply a man in a cloak rather than a man in a cage.

**The Curse  Full Accounting:**
The subject has disclosed the following constraints, which I have verified through observation and divine inquiry:

*The Prohibitions:*
1. **He cannot harm a holy person.** Any individual who serves a divine power  priest, paladin, acolyte, even a farmer who tends a roadside shrine  is beyond his violence. His blade stops mid-swing. His fists will not close. The armor simply will not permit it.

2. **He cannot steal.** Attempts to take what is not freely given end badly. He has been vague about the specifics, but I observed him reach for an unattended coin purse during our first week of travel  testing the boundaries, I assume. His hand stopped three inches from the leather. His fingers trembled. He withdrew, and did not speak for an hour afterward.

3. **He cannot commit acts of cruelty for their own sake.** The armor distinguishes between violence in service of a goal and violence as entertainment. The former is permitted, within limits. The latter is not.

*The Path Forward:*
The prohibitions are only half the curse. The other half is what he must *do* to break it.

Love. Charity. Genuine, uncoerced acts of mercy.

He is not compelled to perform these acts. The armor does not seize his hands and force bread into a beggar's palm. If he walks past a starving child, he suffers no immediate consequence  no pain, no punishment, no tightening of the scales around his chest.

But every act of genuine kindness chips away at the binding. Every moment of real compassion loosens the demon's grip. The curse is a lock, and charity is the key  but no one is forcing him to turn it.

This distinction matters. The armor has not made him good. It has made cruelty impossible and kindness *useful*. What he does with that architecture is his choice.

So far, he has chosen very little.

**The Demon:**
There is something alive in the armor.

The subject calls it a demon with the weary familiarity of a man who has been arguing with the same voice for years. "The bastard," he says. "The parasite." "That smug, sanctimonious *thing*."

The demon speaks to him still. Not often. Not predictably. But when it does, he listens  because ignoring it, he says, produces a headache that lasts for days.

I have not heard the demon myself. I have, however, seen Malanur stop mid-sentence, his eyes going distant, his hand rising to press against the chest plate as if feeling for a heartbeat beneath the scales. In those moments, he is not entirely present. Someone else is borrowing his attention.

When the moment passes, he usually mutters something unflattering under his breath. The demon, apparently, has opinions about everything.

**Analysis:**
The priests promised his greatest enemy would be brought to heel. They did not lie. They simply neglected to mention that his greatest enemy was himself.

His capacity for cruelty. His willingness to steal and kill without moral restraint. His conviction that the throne justified any atrocity committed in its pursuit.

The armor wrapped itself around all of that and set boundaries. It did not make him kind  it simply made unkindness impossible and offered him an incentive to change.

He has not taken the offer. Not yet. He performs the minimum acts of decency required by circumstance and resents every one of them. He hides the armor beneath a black cloak and pretends he is still the man he was before the binding.

But the demon is patient. The curse is patient. And I have begun to suspect that Malanur Blackstar, despite his protests, is changing whether he admits it or not.

The cloak cannot hide everything.

## ENTRY V: THE ROAD HOME
**Source:** Subject's testimony and stated intentions.

He still means to reclaim Castle Ormen.

This is not delusion. He speaks of it with the calm certainty of a man describing a destination rather than a dream. Velara sits on his throne, wearing his title, having murdered everyone who once spoke his name with loyalty. He intends to return. He intends to remove her.

The armor complicates this goal considerably.

Velara, by all accounts, has aligned House Blackstar with one of the Underdark's spider-goddess cults. The new seal on the trade manifests. The purge of the old faith. The timing of her rise. It all points toward Lolth's web.

If Velara is a priestess  or even a favored servant  of a divine power, Malanur cannot touch her. His sword will not swing. His hands will not close around her throat. The armor will stop him at the moment of triumph and hold him there, frozen, while his sister laughs.

He knows this. He has had years to consider it.

"I'll find a way," he told me, adjusting the cloak that never quite sits right over the silver scales. "The curse has rules. Rules have edges. I just need to find where the edge meets her neck."

I wrote that down verbatim. It seemed important.

**Assessment:**
His goal is reclamation. His method is, as yet, undefined. The armor has made cruelty impossible and kindness advantageous  but it has also made his vengeance geometrically more difficult.

He is a man walking toward a locked door, knowing the key might require becoming someone he does not want to be.

I find myself, against professional judgment, hoping he finds a way through.

## ENTRY VI: ONGOING OBSERVATIONS
**Date:** Current

The subject remains a valuable companion. His knowledge of Underdark politics is encyclopedic. His tactical mind is sharp. His sword arm is dependable, provided his enemies have not taken holy orders.

He provides physical security for our party  a function I am ill-suited to perform and Leena cannot be expected to shoulder. When violence finds us, he steps forward with the resignation of a man who has made peace with bloodshed, even as the armor sets limits on whose blood he may shed.

He knows I am documenting him. He finds it amusing  and, I suspect, flattering.

"Writing my eulogy already, Scribe?" he asked last week, watching the quill scratch across the page. The black cloak was pulled tight around his shoulders, as always, though we were indoors and the fire was warm.

"Your biography," I corrected. "Eulogies are for the dead. You are not dead yet."

"Yet," he repeated, and smiled  the thin, knowing smile of a man who has survived things that should have killed him, and expects that pattern to eventually break.

He has begun offering details unprompted. His tactical brilliance during the mountain campaigns. The time he outmaneuvered a Kydorian patrol with only three men. The loyalty his followers showed him, freely given, never coerced.

I recognize vanity when I see it. I also recognize opportunity.

The Archive benefits from willing sources. If his ego opens doors his caution would keep closed, I will not complain.

**Open Questions:**
1. What was the nature of the "unforgivable embarrassment" that caused his disinheritance?
2. What is the demon's true nature? Why is it bound to the armor? What does it want beyond his reformation?
3. What form of love will break the curse? Has he ever been close? Has he ever tried?
4. If Velara is protected by divine favor, how does he intend to circumvent the armor's prohibition?
5. What happened to the priest who survived the journey to the armor's shrine? (I have not asked. I am not certain I want the answer.)

**Status:**
The Codex remains open. The quill is sharpened. The observation continues.

And somewhere beneath the silver scales, hidden under black cloth and bravado, the demon is listening too.
